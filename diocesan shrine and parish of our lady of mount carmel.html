Diocesan Shrine and Parish of Our Lady of Mount Carmel

A historic Marian shrine in Sison that serves as a center of devotion, worship, and spiritual renewal in northern Pangasinan.

The Diocesan Shrine and Parish of Our Lady of Mount Carmel in Sison, Pangasinan is a historic Roman Catholic church dedicated to Our Lady of Mount Carmel, whose feast is celebrated every July 16. Established as a parish in 1898, this shrine has served generations of devotees through regular liturgical services and community outreach under the Diocese of Urdaneta.

History and Establishment

The shrine traces its roots to 1868 as a chapel station, or visita, of San Fabian. It became an independent parish in 1898, formally serving the growing Catholic community in northern Pangasinan.

Over the years, the church has undergone renovations and expansions while preserving its sacred and historical character. Its facade combines simplicity with traditional Catholic design elements, including a prominent bell tower and Marian symbols.

Inside, the sanctuary offers a serene environment for prayer and reflection, with altars and devotional spaces dedicated to Our Lady of Mount Carmel. Its long-standing presence has made it both a historical site and a living center of faith.

Worship and Community Activities

As an active parish, the shrine offers regular daily and Sunday Masses, as well as special celebrations during feast days and liturgical seasons. The feast of Our Lady of Mount Carmel on July 16 is the parish's major highlight, attracting devotees from surrounding towns for processions, prayer services, and communal gatherings.

Beyond liturgical celebrations, the parish promotes youth formation, catechesis, and charitable outreach. Parishioners participate in ministries that support religious education, volunteer work, and community care.

The shrine also serves as a venue for baptisms, weddings, and confirmations, reinforcing its place in the spiritual milestones of the local Catholic community.

Cultural and Religious Significance

The shrine embodies strong Marian devotion, especially in honoring Our Lady of Mount Carmel as protector and guide. This devotion is deeply woven into local culture, shaping not only spiritual practice but also community identity in Sison and nearby municipalities.

Its title reflects Carmelite spirituality, which emphasizes prayer, contemplation, and guidance under Mary's patronage. The parish contributes to Pangasinan's broader religious landscape, complementing other Marian shrines and pilgrimage sites throughout the region.

Through its historical continuity, celebrations, and community programs, the Diocesan Shrine and Parish of Our Lady of Mount Carmel remains both a spiritual anchor and a cultural landmark for residents, pilgrims, and visitors alike.

Key Facts

  • Location: Sison, Pangasinan, Philippines
  • Established: 1898
  • Feast Day: July 16, Feast of Our Lady of Mount Carmel
  • Diocese: Diocese of Urdaneta
  • Type: Diocesan shrine and parish church
